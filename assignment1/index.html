RICH BROWN

web review

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When I arrived at the home page, the loading page caught my attention. After it finished, I focused on the elements on the home page, which is a bit crowded but still looks elegant. Also, the website's scrolling effect is interesting since it has a unique movement that not many other websites have.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Playing with the line that follows the cursor.
  • Trying to figure out which object or element is interactive.
  • Figuring out how to change the background color from each disciplines.
  • Click and explore each video for Brown's previous projects.
  • scrolling up and down the website.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The discipline section of the website because each project has its color. When we move our mouse near or on top of the picture, it can follow our mouse angle. For example, if our mouse is at the picture's bottom left corner, it will automatically tilt, looking like the bottom left corner is the center point.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

I'm playing with the line that follows the cursor and activating the big circle. I'm also moving back and forth on the website to see the transitions between each section and focus more on how it is displayed. But the most common one is changing colors between the projects' background.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

It's very creative, and each result of the work is structured in each section, which makes it accessible. However, it's hard to figure out how to go back to the gallery page if I click on one of them. I love the color choices and the fonts. The transitions are very flowy and smooth when changing each section.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

It communicates through a simple design with a structured layout. The buttons for contact information, biography, and works are easy to navigate. The interactive links allows users to engage deeper with Brown's work by understanding his expertise and allowing people to explore his works without searching from external page.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

It took around 10 minutes to explore the website, see how it works, and examine the features applied to each interactive object. Since it has an exciting layout and design, I tend to look back around 2-3 times to play with the transitions of the design.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The landing page has all the essential informations that users can use to get contacts. The gallery that shows Brown's current work speaks that user needs to explore each work to know Brown's style of design. By placing the archive works he did before, it can help user to view the work easily.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

He links his social media accounts for users to know Brown on other platforms, mentions many collaborations and projects he has done before, and lists all the awards he received as a UI/UX freelance designer for over two decades. The "B" logo of the home page also shows an interview with questions and answers.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

Using these references allows us to connect by following his social media accounts and help users to stay updated with the latest activities or projects. It allows us to explore Brown's work more and see reviews by the clients who have collaborated or worked with him before.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Social media can make us feel more connected with Brown since he has many platforms for clients to explore his work and biography. The videos in each work made us curious about what Brown does in different works he did before. It builds trust and acknowledgment for the client as well.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

There is no back button whenever I open any work to see more details. Even if there is, it's confusing and invisible about how to get back to the main page. The Q&A part on the home page could be more readable since the words are too small, and there is no separate section for questions or answers.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

I like how unique and smooth the transition is when we scroll up and down through the web. This dragging effect makes it more flowy and relaxed. Additionally, I enjoyed the changing colors with each work, which made it exciting and showed that each work had its style and design.
